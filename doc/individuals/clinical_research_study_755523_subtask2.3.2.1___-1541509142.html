  • "CT scanning is used in multi-modality imaging for attenuation correction, anatomic localization and/or acquisition of additional diagnostic information. A method for organ and tissue doses estimation from diagnostic chest CT, also applicable to CT units of multi-modality systems, will be developed in Subtask 2.1.2. Using the same MC platform, the current subtask aims for a patientspecific organ dose estimation from a) cone-beam CT scanners used in multi-modality systems and b) CT scanners dedicated to attenuation correction and anatomic localization. This will focus on the most frequently used chest CT protocols and those with the highest contribution to the collective dose to patients in PET/CT and SPECT/CT identified in Subtask 2.3.1. A retrospective study will be conducted based on chest CT examinations performed on at least 200 adults. As in 2.1.2, using anonymised patient CT images as input volume, 3D patient models will be created for each patient. 3D dose distributions will be generated for the most important CT protocols used in PET/CT and SPECT/CT. MC simulations will be performed using an equipment-specific and patient-specific Monte Carlo code (ImpactMC). At least 5 modern CT scanners will be modelled. The scanners' geometry, X-ray spectrum, composition and dimensions of the filters will be taken into account for the simulation. Normalized to CTDIvol organ absorbed doses will be determined for all radiosensitive organs within the primary exposed volume. The latter data will serve as a basis for patient-specific organ doses estimates, taking into account patientsâ€™ body size. The resulting dose estimations will be essential novel information for dose optimisation of chest PET/CT and SPECT/CT procedures in 2.3.2.3, benefit-to-risk evaluation, epidemiological research and for modelling of radiation-induced cancer risk."
