  • dcterms:description "OntoMEDIRAD is an application ontology designed to specify the vocabulary used in the description of experimental data used in the EU MEDIRAD project (H2020, Grant Agreement NÂ°755523).
    The objective of the MEDIRAD project (title: Implications of Medical Low Dose Radiation Exposure) is to "enhance the scientific bases and practice of radiation protection (RP) in the medical field. In particular, this is achieved by focusing on: 1) improvement of organ dose estimation and registration in order to a) inform clinical practice, optimise doses and set recommendations and b) provide adequate dosimetry for clinical-epidemiological studies of effects of medical radiation."
    The design of OntoMEDIRAD aims at ensuring that experimental data collected during the MEDIRAD project, consisting primarily of medical images and related estimated dose data, is defined in an explicit and well-documented way.
    It contains several extracts of existing ontologies, aligned onto the Basic Formal Ontology (BFO) used as a foundational ontology. The sources of the main extracts are: FMA (human anatomy), ChEBI (chemical entities), SNMI (radionuclides), IAO (information artefacts), MPATH (Mouse Pathology), PATO (phenotypic qualities), DCM (DICOM standard)."     @en
